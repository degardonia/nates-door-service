Recognize the Problem

Signs Your Opener Needs Attention

Remote or Wall Button Not Responding

If pressing the button does nothing — no light, no motor sound — the opener may have lost power or the logic board may be failing.

Door Reverses Before Closing

If the door starts to close then immediately opens again, the safety sensor alignment is usually the culprit — a quick, inexpensive fix.

Grinding, Clicking, or Straining Noises

Unusual sounds from the motor unit often mean worn gears, a stripped drive, or the opener straining against a mechanical issue like a broken spring.

Opens or Closes on Its Own

A door that moves without being triggered usually points to frequency interference, a stuck remote button, or a failing circuit board.

Motor Runs but Door Doesn't Move

You hear the motor running, but nothing happens. This often means the disconnect cord was pulled, or the drive gear or trolley is stripped.

Opener Is More Than 10–15 Years Old

Older openers lack modern safety features like auto-reverse and rolling code security. Upgrading is often more economical than repeated repairs.
