Is It Time?

Signs It's Time for a New Door

Door Is 15–20+ Years Old

Older doors lose insulation efficiency, develop rust, and start needing frequent repairs. At some point, replacement is more cost-effective than continued patching.

Major Impact or Structural Damage

When a door takes a serious hit — from a vehicle or a severe storm — the panels, tracks, and frame can be compromised beyond what individual repairs can fix.

Poor Insulation & High Energy Bills

Uninsulated or poorly insulated doors are a major source of heat loss in winter and heat gain in summer. A new insulated door can make a noticeable difference in comfort and utility costs.

Repairs Are Adding Up

If you've had multiple repairs in the past couple of years and something new keeps breaking, you're likely spending more keeping an old door alive than a replacement would cost.

Curb Appeal & Home Value

Your garage door is one of the most visible elements of your home's exterior. A new door is consistently one of the highest-ROI home improvement projects, especially before a sale.

Outdated Safety Features

Older doors may lack modern auto-reverse safety features. Newer doors and openers meet current safety standards, which is especially important if children or pets are present.
